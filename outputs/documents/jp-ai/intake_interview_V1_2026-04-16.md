# JP Intake Interview — Ozum AI-OS
**Version 1 · 2026-04-16**

Purpose: understand what JP actually does day-to-day so his dashboard reflects HIS mental model, not Jano's. Don't show him windows or mockups yet — this is a listening session.

## How to run it

- 60–90 min call, video if possible so you can read reactions
- Spanish, informal — let him ramble, the tangents are where the real signal lives
- Don't take the questions in strict order; follow his energy
- Record it (with permission) so you can re-listen for details you missed
- Your job is to understand, not to pitch. Do not describe the dashboard yet.

**Opening line:** *"Quiero entender a fondo cómo trabajas una semana normal en Ozum — qué haces tú, qué se te atora, qué te quita tiempo, qué decisiones tomas. Con eso voy a diseñar la herramienta. Entre más crudo y específico me cuentes, mejor sale."*

---

## Block 1 — A typical week

- Walk me through last week, day by day. What did you actually do?
- What time did you start? What time did you stop?
- How many meetings? With who — clients, team, vendors, internal?
- What did you do between meetings?
- Which day was the "best" week day and why? Which was the worst?

**Follow-ups depending on answers:**
- If he mentions a lot of firefighting → "What was on fire? Who brought it to you?"
- If he mentions client calls → "What did they ask for? Were you prepared or scrambling?"
- If he mentions admin/reports → "Who consumes those reports? What happens if you skip one?"
- If he mentions WhatsApp a lot → "Which chats are business-critical? How do you keep track of commitments made there?"

---

## Block 2 — What Ozum actually sells

- In one sentence, what does Ozum do?
- What are the 3–5 types of engagements you sell? (corporate event, incentive trip, hybrid, etc.)
- What's the typical ticket size for each type? Range?
- What's a "big" win vs a "normal" win?
- How long is a typical sales cycle from first contact to signed contract?
- How long from signed to event day?
- How long from event day to cash in the bank?

**Follow-ups:**
- If cycles vary wildly → "What makes the fast ones fast and the slow ones slow?"
- If he says "it depends" → "Give me the last 3 deals you closed and walk me through each."
- If he mentions recurring clients → "What % of revenue is recurring vs new logo?"

---

## Block 3 — The deal lifecycle (this is the core)

Walk me through a deal from the moment you hear about it to the moment the money lands:

- Where do leads come from? (referral, inbound, outbound, events, partners)
- Who does the first qualifying call — you or someone else?
- What info do you need to collect before you can quote?
- How do you put a proposal together? Who helps? How long does it take?
- What's the quote/proposal format? PDF, deck, spreadsheet, Notion, verbal?
- How often does the client push back on price/scope? What do you change?
- Who signs the contract? What's the paperwork step?
- Who runs the event once it's signed? Are you involved in production or do you hand off?
- Who invoices? Who chases payment?

**Follow-ups:**
- If he mentions a CRM → "What do you use? What do you love/hate about it?"
- If he doesn't → "Where does deal info live today? Spreadsheet? His head?"
- If a step feels manual → "How many hours a week does that eat?"
- If handoffs exist → "Where do balls get dropped between steps?"
- If there's a proposal generator → "What's the hardest part about writing a proposal?"
- If he mentions partners/referrers → "Do you owe them commissions? How do you track?"

---

## Block 4 — Clients

- Who are your top 5 clients by revenue? (just names/industries, no need for numbers)
- How did you win each of them?
- When a client calls, what do you need to remember about them in 10 seconds?
- Do you keep notes per client? Where?
- How often do you follow up with clients between engagements?
- Who at Ozum owns the client relationship — you alone, shared, account managers?

**Follow-ups:**
- If notes are scattered → "What's the cost of forgetting a detail? Tell me a story."
- If he has favorite clients → "What makes them easy? What makes others painful?"
- If he mentions churn → "Why do clients leave? Who do they go to?"

---

## Block 5 — Vendors and the supply side

- What kinds of vendors do you work with? (venues, DJs, caterers, AV, travel ops, speakers, decor, transport, hotels)
- How many vendors do you have in your rolodex right now?
- How do you pick one for a given event?
- Do you rate them? How? Where?
- When a vendor flakes or underdelivers, what happens?
- Do you negotiate rates? Do you have preferred pricing with anyone?

**Follow-ups:**
- If vendor mgmt is informal → "How do you decide between two vendors offering the same thing?"
- If he has preferred vendors → "What earned them that status?"
- If there's been a recent disaster → "Walk me through what went wrong and how you'd prevent it."

---

## Block 6 — The team

- Who reports to you directly? What do they each own?
- Who do you report to? Owner, board, nobody?
- How many people total at Ozum? Full-time vs contractor?
- Who makes what decisions — pricing, hiring, vendor choice, scope changes?
- What are your 1-on-1 / team meeting rhythms?
- Who's your right hand when you're underwater?

**Follow-ups:**
- If he sounds bottlenecked → "What only you can do vs what you keep doing out of habit?"
- If team is small → "If you hired one person tomorrow, what role?"
- If there's a co-founder/partner → "How do you divide work?"

---

## Block 7 — Tools he uses today

- What's on your home screen / bookmark bar?
- Email: which platform? How many unread right now?
- Calendar: Google, Outlook? Who sees it?
- Chat: WhatsApp? Slack? Something else?
- Docs: Google Drive, Dropbox, Notion, OneDrive?
- Spreadsheets: for what? Which ones would die if they got corrupted?
- CRM or pipeline tracker: what, if anything?
- Accounting: who runs it? What tool?
- Design tools: who makes proposals? Canva, Figma, PowerPoint?
- Any AI tools he already uses? ChatGPT? Claude? For what?

**Follow-ups:**
- For every tool → "What do you open it to do?" and "What's the worst part of using it?"
- If he says "just WhatsApp + Excel" → "Which sheet is the most important? Can you screen-share?"
- If he has a CRM → "What % of deals are actually logged there? Honestly."

---

## Block 8 — Where time leaks and balls drop

- If I watched you for a week, what would I see you doing that you wish you didn't have to?
- What gets forgotten? What do you have to re-do because someone dropped something?
- Where does info live in someone's head instead of a system?
- What meetings could be async?
- What do you do on Sunday night so Monday doesn't suck?

**Follow-ups:**
- Every pain → "How often? Weekly? Monthly? Per event?"
- If he mentions reporting → "Who asks for what, how often, and in what format?"
- If he mentions status updates → "Who chases you vs who do you chase?"

---

## Block 9 — Money

- How do you know if Ozum had a good month? What number do you look at?
- How far out is the pipeline visible? 1 month? 1 quarter? 1 year?
- What's the margin on a typical event? Do you track margin per event or just topline?
- Cash collection: how long from event to paid? Any chronic late payers?
- How do you forecast the next quarter?
- Who else sees the money numbers — you only, owner, team?

**Follow-ups:**
- If numbers feel loose → "When was the last time a financial surprise hit you? What did you wish you'd seen earlier?"
- If he tracks margin → "In a spreadsheet? How fresh is it?"
- If cash is a pain → "How much AR is outstanding right now, roughly?"

---

## Block 10 — Decisions he makes

- What decisions do you make every week that you wish were easier?
- What do you want data on that you don't have?
- What do you currently decide based on gut that you wish you could decide based on data?
- When you make a bad call, where does the signal usually come from afterward?
- What's a decision you've been putting off?

**Follow-ups:**
- If he mentions pricing → "How do you set price today? What would help?"
- If he mentions hiring → "What's the trigger to hire? Revenue? Burnout? Specific skill gap?"
- If he mentions scope changes → "Who has authority to say yes to a client mid-event?"

---

## Block 11 — What winning looks like

- At end of a great week, what did you accomplish?
- At end of a great quarter, what's different?
- What does Ozum look like in 2 years if everything goes right?
- What would free up the most time for you personally?
- If I gave you one superpower for this business, what would it be?

**Follow-ups:**
- If vision is vague → "Revenue target? Team size? Deal count?"
- If personal → "What do you want to spend MORE time doing?"

---

## Block 12 — The dashboard wedge (only at the end)

Now — and only now — tell him you're building a single screen he'll open every morning. Ask:

- If you opened one screen every morning before your first coffee, what 5 things would you want to see on it?
- What 3 things do you check multiple times a day today?
- What alerts would genuinely help vs just add noise?
- What would you want to be able to do FROM that screen without switching tabs? (reply to a client, mark a deal won, approve a vendor quote, etc.)

## Red flags to listen for

- "I'll send it later" → info lives in his head, dashboard must capture it
- "Depends" used a lot → there are hidden rules; dig them out
- Laughs nervously about a tool/process → it's broken, he's embarrassed
- Mentions the same name repeatedly → that person is a dependency, model their role
- Long pauses on a question → either unclear or sensitive, come back to it
